The Problem

There’s a kind of pressure that doesn’t get talked about much.

You’re leading an organisation of fifty, a hundred, or two hundred people. A vendor has sent you a major proposal, an investor is asking about your AI strategy. The decisions are significant. The spend is real. Your political capital is at risk.

You could call a Big 4 firm but you’re not a big enough for them to give you a senior partner. You could bring in a niche development shop, but they’ll solve the technical problem without touching the strategic one.

With deadlines fast approaching, and no way to side-step the scenario, the weight of the decision is crippling you.

That’s why Corbelle exists.
