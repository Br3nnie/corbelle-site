Experience in rooms where the wrong answer carries huge consequences.

Hello, my name is Brendan. I have spent my entire career at the intersection of data, technology, and leadership. I step into the room for rock-versus-hard-place decisions, for moments when credibility is on the line and the wrong choice means career-limiting consequences.

I was Head of Data & BI, then Digital Strategy Lead at Pantheon, one of the world’s largest private equity fund of funds. Before that, I served as Head of Data at Shawbrook Bank, a PE-backed lender navigating rapid growth. Backed by a Big 4 foundation across multi-industry sectors (media, construction, travel), I bring significant experience in sectors that never forgive expensive mistakes.

I recognise a bad technology decision long before it happens. More importantly, I know exactly how it feels when you carry the crushing weight of that choice as an executive without the right partner in the room.

Isolation defines the top of small & mid-market businesses. A Board sits above you, teams wait below, and Executive peers surround you…the pressure never ends. Vendors hunt the contract. Your team demands your confidence. Yet, no one in your immediate circle is in a position to simply tell you the truth.

At this level, your organisation operates at a difficult scale, too large for boutique firms to serve effectively, yet too small to justify the exorbitant engagement fees that unlock senior talent at the big consultancies. Instead, you fall into what I call ‘the messy middle’, left to figure it out alone.

I am currently completing an MSc in Servant Leadership, focusing my research on generational dynamics in the modern workplace. As a geriatric Millennial, I sit simultaneously at both ends of the generational divide; coaching the leaders who manage Gen Z while researching how the largest workforce demographic shifts our approach to work.

I remain driven by testing my limits. Most recently, I ran 220 kilometres in 5 days across the Jordanian desert, a milestone that sits alongside completing IRONMAN events, marathons up mountains, and cycling the length of the UK. The crossover to the corporate world is clear: the executives I partner with carry immense decisions they never felt fully trained for, inside an environment moving faster than corporate playbooks cover. I know what it takes to show up for an objective that feels daunting, anxiety-inducing, and inevitable.

My philosophy in this work centres on a simple concept: being a Leadership Caddie™. A caddie walks alongside the player, reads the course, and offers the right club at the exact right moment but never plays the shot. That always remains the player’s responsibility.

Corbelle deliberately limits the client roster to a select few at any given time. This is entirely intentional. What I offer isn’t a packaged service, a cookie-cutter framework, or a standardized report. It is a rigorous thinking partnership. And a true partnership requires undivided presence, absolute attention, and unbroken continuity.

Our relationship begins with a conversation, not a contract. The very first thing we will discover together is whether our minds and dynamics are the right match. I routinely turn away projects that fall outside of this boutique model. But the select engagements I do take on, I execute with absolute, unwavering intensity.
